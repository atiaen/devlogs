The beginning (26/08/2024)

So I still don't have name for this project but I have an idea and some systems I want to make. Below is the list of systems:

  1. Cooking System
    Ingredient Management: Players manage and unlock various ingredients, each with different qualities and preparation methods (frying, grilling, baking, etc.). (In progress) Dish Creation: Players combine ingredients to create dishes. The system allows for flexibility in how dishes are made, enabling creative combinations. (In progress) Preparation Methods: Players can choose how to prepare ingredients (e.g., fry, grill, bake), adding another layer of depth to the cooking process.
  2. Customer Interaction System
    Customer Desires: Each customer has a desired ingredient, and players must incorporate this into their dish. The desire is not quality-specific, allowing for flexibility in satisfying the customer. (In progress) Customer Moods: Customer moods are influenced by various factors (e.g., weather, random events), adding unpredictability to customer behavior. Order Changes: Customers may change their orders or desires, adding a dynamic challenge for players.
  3. Economic System
    Ingredient Pricing: The cost of ingredients and the potential for unlocking more expensive/complex ones as players progress. Dish Pricing: Pricing of dishes could be determined by either the customers or based on the total cost of ingredients plus a percentage markup. Profit Management: Players must balance costs, pricing, and customer satisfaction to maintain profitability.
  4. Competition System
    AI Restaurants: Competing against AI-controlled restaurants, with the risk of being shut down if ranked low for consecutive periods. Leaderboard Dynamics: A ranking system where the bottom 3 restaurants for a certain period (days/weeks/months/years) face consequences like closure or demolition. Market Influence: Playersâ€™ performance affects their standing in the market, encouraging strategic play.
  5. Dynamic Event System
    Weather Effects: Weather conditions can influence customer moods and behavior, adding another layer of unpredictability. Random Events: Unplanned events that can change customer desires or restaurant operations, requiring players to adapt quickly.
